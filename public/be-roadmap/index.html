Strategies for success

How to Use Abdul Bari's Course Effectively

‚úÖ DO:

  • Watch at 1.25-1.5x speed
  • Pause and code along with examples
  • Take notes in your own words
  • Ask yourself "Could I explain this to someone?"
  • Use as a reference when stuck on problems
  • Focus on understanding concepts, not memorizing code

‚ùå DON'T:

  • Try to watch every single video
  • Watch passively without coding
  • Get stuck on mathematical proofs (unless you enjoy them)
  • Aim for 100% completion
  • Move on without implementing the data structure yourself

Problem-Solving Strategy

When Starting a Problem:

  1. Read carefully, understand constraints
  2. Think of brute force solution first (even if slow)
  3. Identify which data structure/algorithm fits
  4. Code your solution
  5. Test with examples (including edge cases)
  6. Analyze time/space complexity

When Stuck (>30 minutes):

  1. Re-read the problem
  2. Look at hints on LeetCode
  3. Still stuck after 15 more min? Watch NeetCode video solution
  4. Understand the solution completely
  5. Close the video and code it yourself from scratch
  6. Try the same problem again tomorrow (spaced repetition)

Effective Practice Habits

The 30-Minute Rule: Struggle for 30 minutes on your own. This struggle is where learning happens. Don't look at solutions too quickly.

Spaced Repetition: Review solved problems after: 1 day, 3 days, 1 week. Keep a "mistake journal".

Pattern Recognition: After 5-10 problems, identify patterns (e.g., "Two-pointer problems usually involve sorted arrays").

Quality Over Quantity: Better to solve 10 problems deeply than 50 superficially.

"I'm Moving Too Slowly"

Symptoms: Spent 3 weeks on one topic, or only solved 20 problems in 2 months.

Solutions:

  • ‚úÖ Speed up video playback (1.5x)
  • ‚úÖ Skip advanced sections
  • ‚úÖ Focus more on practice, less on watching
  • ‚úÖ Set a timer: 2 weeks max per major topic
  • ‚úÖ Move on even if you don't feel 100% ready

"Problems Are Too Hard"

Symptoms: Can't solve any Easy problems, feel overwhelmed.

Solutions:

  • ‚úÖ Go back to Abdul Bari lectures for that topic
  • ‚úÖ Start with the easiest problems (NeetCode marks them)
  • ‚úÖ Watch NeetCode video solutions, then try yourself
  • ‚úÖ Implement the data structure from scratch again
  • ‚úÖ Be patient - it takes time to build problem-solving muscles

"I'm Burned Out"

Symptoms: Dreading practice sessions, can't focus.

Solutions:

  • ‚úÖ Take 3-5 days completely off
  • ‚úÖ Reduce hours to 5-8/week temporarily
  • ‚úÖ Build something fun (not for practice, just for joy)
  • ‚úÖ Review your why: why are you doing this?

Common questions

About the Approach

Q: Why C++ instead of JavaScript?
A: JavaScript hides too much (no pointers, automatic memory). C++ teaches you CS fundamentals while being practical.

Q: Why 4 months instead of 6?
A: 6 months is overkill. After 4 months of focused work, you'll be ready for most interviews and can move to System Design.

Q: Should I really skip parts of the course I paid for?
A: Yes! The goal is learning, not completion. Skip advanced proofs.

About Practice

Q: Should I solve problems in order or by topic?
A: By topic. Learn linked lists ‚Üí solve linked list problems.

Q: What if I can't solve a problem after 30 minutes?
A: Look at hints. Still stuck? Watch solution, understand it, then code it yourself.

Mindset for success

Why This Approach Works

  • Fundamentals First: Learn the "why" behind the tools.
  • Active Learning: 40% learning, 60% practicing. Struggle = learning.
  • Depth Over Breadth: Deeply understand 150 problems > superficially know 500.
  • Spaced Repetition: Review after 1d, 3d, 1w to retain knowledge.

The Mindset You Need

  • Growth Mindset: Difficulty = opportunity to learn.
  • Long-term Thinking: 4 months now = decades of opportunities.
  • Embrace the Struggle: If it's easy, you're not learning.
  • Consistency Over Intensity: 1 hour daily > 7 hours on Sunday.
